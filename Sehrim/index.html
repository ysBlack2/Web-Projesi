Gaza's Beach

Al-Quzai'ya Beach, which boasts gorgeous white sand and crystal-clear water, is one of Gaza's most well-liked and highly ranked beaches, according to a plethora of sources, including travel guides and firsthand reports from tourists.

Other noteworthy beach locations are Beach No. 9, which boasts breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea, and Al-Saraya Beach, a well-liked and lively meeting spot for locals. Though these beaches have received high marks, whenever you visit any area of the Gaza Strip, you should also take safety considerations and travel advice into account.

The Great Mosque

The Great Mosque of Gaza, also known as the Great Omari Mosque, was the largest and oldest mosque in the Gaza Strip, Palestine, located in Gaza City.

Believed to stand on the site of an ancient Philistine temple, the site was used by the Byzantines to erect a church in the 5th century. After the Muslim conquest in the 7th century, it was transformed into a mosque. Described as "beautiful" by Ibn Battuta, the Great Mosque's minaret was toppled in an earthquake in 1033. In 1149, the Crusaders built a large church. It was mostly destroyed by the Ayyubids in 1187, and then rebuilt as a mosque by the Mamluks in the early 13th century.
